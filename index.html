Overview:

Search bars are used across multiple web and mobile-based applications. From helping users find specific videos on Youtube, to helping social media users find specific accounts, to helping internet explorers find the perfect article for their topic of interest. While their implementations may vary, all search bars share one crucial detail:

Search bars are only effective if they are usable and accessible

The following case study analyzes and addresses inconsistencies across search bar implemenation that prevents their usuability and hinders their accessibility.

More specifically I:

  1. Analyzed the input and output states of search bars across three applications
  2. Selected one of the three aforermentioned applications and created state models for both mouse and keyboard users
  3. Created a redesigned search bar that addresses some of the inconsistencies I noticed during the analyses
  4. Created a state model for the redesigned component
  5. Reflected on my learnings throughout this process
